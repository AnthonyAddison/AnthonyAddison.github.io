2. Discuss the history and origin of Bagels#

Bagels have a long and distinguished history, beginning in Poland in the 16th century, when Jews were emancipated from the ghettos by local authorities and given the freedom to own property. But the traditional recipe for making bagels was actually brought to the United States by Jewish immigrants in the 19th century. Initially, bagels were a symbol of Jewish emigration and were sold on the streets of New York city. Eventually, bagels grew in popularity with the New Yorkers and spread to the rest of the US.

Many different variations of bagels were invented in the 20th century, such as sesame bagels, garlic bagels, and even blueberry bagels. These variations have made the bagel the ultimate snack food, enjoyed all over the world. Bagels are still made using the same traditional recipe as they were centuries ago, with a plain dough ball that is boiled and then baked.
