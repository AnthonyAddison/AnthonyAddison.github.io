Q4: What is the benefit of using stop-loss orders to protect against downside risk? A4: Stop-loss orders are a great way to protect against downside risk in the stock market. These orders are limit orders that automatically trigger a sale of a stock if it reaches a certain predetermined price. As such, they help investors manage their risk and ensure that losses are minimized if a stock’s price drops due to market fluctuations.

Q5: How does cost-efficient investing through dollar cost averaging work? A5: Cost-efficient investing through dollar cost averaging involves making a fixed investment in a particular stock or ETF on a periodic basis. Since this method often involves buying more shares when the price is low and fewer shares when the price is high, it helps investors average out the cost of their investments and may also reduce the likelihood of incurring losses.
